# Candidate Destination Poster Grammar

## Status

`candidate`: one source artifact plus one forward output is insufficient for promotion.

## Function

Compress a place into one dominant identity, widen the promise through subordinate context, and bridge desire to a feasible travel action.

## Required Relations

| Element | Role | Required Relation |
|---|---|---|
| destination anchor | immediate identity | dominates supporting scenery at thumbnail size |
| context field | locate or broaden promise | supports but does not compete with the anchor |
| destination naming | verbal recognition | shares the image field while remaining legible |
| directional cue | movement and access | carries attention toward travel mode or action |
| action copy | feasible next step | remains visible but subordinate to desire |

## Variables

- anchor: landform, architecture, wildlife, vehicle, human gesture, or symbolic object
- density: sparse scene or controlled collage
- directional device: vehicle motion, route, light, wind, or perspective
- verbal sequence: destination/promise/action or feeling/destination/departure
- source of truth: code, vector file, layered design, or timeline

## Constraints

- Dense detail requires a large quiet field or simplified silhouette.
- Integrated typography cannot lose legibility at delivery size.
- The selected source of truth must permit the expected revision style.
- Source-specific objects and arrangements do not become invariants.

## Counterexample And Limit

The source uses a dense collage while the transfer output uses a sparse landscape. This supports the functional relation but disproves any claim that collage density itself is invariant.
