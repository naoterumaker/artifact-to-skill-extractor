# Grammar Pattern

## Identity

- Pattern ID: `x-mechanism-article-001`
- Function: move a reader from an active pain to an evidence-scoped replacement mechanism
- Intended experience: recognition, causal reframe, comprehension, justified next action
- Status: `candidate`

## Eligible Context

- Medium: Japanese X Article
- Audience: reader experiencing a specific production or decision problem
- Objective: pre-publication readability and useful mechanism explanation
- Tool/source-of-truth assumptions: Markdown draft with a separate evidence ledger

## Elements And Relations

| Element | Role | Required Relation | Variable Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| active pain | establish relevance | must name a current situation rather than a generic aspiration | observation, failure, costly workaround |
| causal challenge | create reframe | must challenge one assumption implied by the pain | contrast, question, correction |
| replacement mechanism | explain | must answer the challenged assumption with evidence-scoped logic | stages, principles, decision rules |
| reasoning units | make inspectable | each section must do a distinct job | three or more distinct functions; no fixed count |
| objection and constraint | preserve trust | must bound when the mechanism does not apply | limitation, counterexample, requirement |
| provenance and action | enable next step | action strength must not exceed evidence or reader commitment | inspect, try, compare, adopt |

## Selection Rules

- Use the strongest claim whose certainty does not exceed the evidence.
- Choose the body shape from the mechanism: stages for temporal processes, principles for value rules, mistakes for recurring failures, and decision rules for branches.
- Use a numerical hook only when the number is independently traceable and scoped.

## Constraints And Invalid Combinations

- Do not pair a qualified body with an absolute title.
- Do not use several sections that restate the same promise.
- Do not describe a pre-publication rubric as live X performance.
- Do not retain distinctive source wording, cadence, examples, or title formula.

## Transfer Prediction

- New context: a different source-backed article about a non-AI operational mechanism
- Expected preserved relation: pain, causal challenge, mechanism, boundaries, provenance, action
- Expected changed expression: title, examples, voice, body shape, evidence type, and CTA
