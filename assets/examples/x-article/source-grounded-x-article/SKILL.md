# Source-Grounded X Article

## Status

Experimental. The method is derived from one positive X Article example and a forward test; keep claims bounded until broader evidence and live analytics exist.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the target reader, desired state change, source rights, and CTA.
2. Extract source claims, evidence, uncertainty, and provenance before drafting.
3. Choose one reader pain and one assumption to challenge.
4. Build a distinct replacement mechanism using `references/method.md`.
5. Draft a compact title, opening, body map, mechanism sections, objection handling, and attributed close.
6. Check every number and causal claim against the source ledger.
7. Run anti-clone and X-readability checks before delivery.

## Output

- title
- article body with scan-friendly headings and paragraphs
- source attribution or link
- claim caveats where required
- a brief evidence ledger when factual claims are material

## Boundary And Quality Rules

- Do not reproduce distinctive source wording or structure mechanically.
- Do not invent metrics, testimonials, algorithm facts, or guaranteed outcomes.
- Do not describe a pre-publication quality score as real impression performance.
- Ask for a lawful source or switch to hypothesis mode when evidence is missing.

## Evaluation

Evaluate a new article for intent, functional structure, claim discipline, X readability, and anti-clone behavior. Treat impressions as an unverified outcome until post-publication analytics exist.
