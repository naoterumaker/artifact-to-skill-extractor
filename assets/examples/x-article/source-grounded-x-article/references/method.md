# Method

## Project Doctrine

These are rules for this generated system, not recovered claims about the source creator:

- Preserve evidence integrity when it conflicts with a more spectacular hook.
- Preserve attribution and reader autonomy when they conflict with appearing wholly original.
- Explain the mechanism rather than repeating a promise.
- Never present a pre-publication quality score as live X performance.

## Claim Decision

```text
WHEN a title or body claim is selected
IF the claim is observed and adequately sourced
DO state it within that evidence scope
VERIFY every material number and causal statement
ELSE attribute, qualify, omit, or request stronger evidence
```

## Functional Sequence

Use this as a relationship map, not a fixed wording template:

```text
specific reader pain
  -> challenge one default assumption
  -> introduce a source-grounded replacement mechanism
  -> preview distinct reasoning units
  -> explain mechanism and constraints
  -> resolve the main objection
  -> attribute evidence and offer one next action
```

Choose `principles`, `stages`, `mistakes`, or `decision rules` based on the material. Do not force every topic into three principles.

## Evidence Ladder

Label claims internally before drafting:

1. `observed`: directly present in a permitted source
2. `attributed`: a source's claim that has not been independently verified
3. `inferred`: a reasoned interpretation
4. `hypothesis`: a proposition that needs testing

Only observed evidence supports unqualified factual wording. Attribute or qualify the rest. Never fabricate a quantitative uplift to strengthen the hook.

## Article Checks

- Opening pain identifies a real reader and situation.
- Reframing changes the reader's model, not merely the wording.
- Each body section performs a different reasoning function.
- Examples and evidence come from the current input, not the extraction source.
- Paragraphs are short enough to scan on mobile.
- The title promises only what the body delivers.
- The CTA is proportional and preserves provenance.
- No distinctive source phrase, exact numerical promise, or signature cadence is reused.

## Impression Objective

Pre-publication proxies include relevance, early clarity, curiosity, scanability, evidence density, and a low-friction next action. They do not guarantee impressions. After publication, capture reach, dwell proxies, bookmarks, shares, profile actions, and link actions where available; use those results to revise the method.
