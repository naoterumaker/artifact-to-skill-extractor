# Compiler Design Doctrine

These rules are the production constitution of `artifact-to-skill-extractor`. Their provenance is owner review and repeated implementation correction; treat them as user-confirmed for this repository, not universal laws.

## Priority Rules

1. **Evidence over plausibility.** When a broad explanation conflicts with available evidence, narrow the claim or keep it as a hypothesis.
2. **Production function over medium label.** A platform is an adapter and constraint set, not the abstraction itself.
3. **System over template.** When output quality depends on values, grammar, tools, and evaluation, compile the full system rather than a surface template.
4. **Native representation over universal schema.** Use prose, tables, JSON, YAML, code, timelines, or graphs according to the information's role.
5. **Executable decisions over descriptive labels.** A function label is incomplete until context, evidence, rule, action, verifier, and fallback are defined.
6. **Comparison over single-example certainty.** A single artifact may seed a hypothesis but cannot establish doctrine or causality.
7. **Capability before tool name.** Decompose required operations and constraints before selecting OSS, CLI, MCP, API, SDK, or skill.
8. **Explicit agency over accidental automation.** Assign judgment, execution, approval, and recovery to human, model, rule, or tool intentionally.
9. **Evaluation before promotion.** Freeze the target before generation and test on unseen cases; do not promote from self-description.
10. **Honest scope over impressive status.** Keep a working skill experimental when coverage, independence, or real-world outcomes remain weak.

## Anti-Goals

- Do not infer a creator's philosophy from one attractive artifact.
- Do not call serialization abstraction.
- Do not treat a list of tools as a toolchain.
- Do not turn every correction into a permanent global rule.
- Do not use model fluency or pre-publication scores as proof of platform performance.
- Do not hide unresolved contradictions to produce a cleaner narrative.

## Tensions

| Tension | Default priority | Exception |
|---|---|---|
| generality vs evidence | evidence | broaden only after transfer support |
| autonomy vs control | explicit approval boundary | automate low-risk deterministic work |
| speed vs traceability | traceability for core rules | allow minimal mode with declared omissions |
| fidelity vs originality | functional fidelity plus expressive distance | close derivatives only with rights and explicit intent |
| system depth vs context cost | progressive disclosure | load the full system for creative/hybrid compilation |
