# Doctrine Induction

Doctrine captures value priorities that govern choices. It is not a list of aesthetic adjectives.

## Doctrine Components

- intended human or system change
- philosophy and ethical stance
- value priorities and conflict rules
- relationship to audience or user
- accepted forms of evidence
- experience priorities over time
- agency and human-review boundaries
- quality doctrine and tolerances
- anti-goals
- distribution and lifecycle stance

## Executable Form

Weak:

```text
minimal, premium, user-first
```

Operational:

```text
When novelty conflicts with comprehension, preserve comprehension.
Use motion only when it explains causality, hierarchy, or state change.
Do not manufacture urgency; require an observable deadline or scarcity condition.
Give the recipient enough evidence to decide rather than treating them only as a persuasion target.
```

Each statement must identify the conflict or condition, preferred value, observable consequence, and exception when one exists.

## Evidence Gate

One artifact cannot verify doctrine. It may only produce low-confidence candidates. Stronger doctrine evidence includes:

- repeated choices across an eligible longitudinal corpus
- revisions moving in the same direction
- accepted/rejected alternatives
- creator explanation consistent with observed choices
- successful prediction on held-out work

Store one doctrine rule per record with its type, owner, support, counterexamples, scope, confidence, and prediction in `assets/templates/doctrine-hypothesis.md`.

## Creator Doctrine Versus Project Doctrine

Keep these separate:

- **Recovered creator doctrine:** inferred from creator evidence and subject to confidence limits.
- **Project doctrine:** explicitly supplied by the user or agreed for the new system.
- **Platform constraints:** externally imposed rules, not values.
- **Model preference:** not evidence and must not be disguised as doctrine.

The new system may use an explicit project doctrine even when the source creator's doctrine is not recoverable.

## Promotion

Promote a doctrine rule only when it predicts or explains multiple eligible decisions without ignoring material counterexamples. Narrow it by medium, audience, genre, or objective when exceptions cluster.
