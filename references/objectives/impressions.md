# Impressions Objective

Impressions are a distribution outcome, not an intrinsic property of an artifact. The skill can optimize evidence-backed conditions but cannot guarantee delivery volume.

## Metric Contract

Define:

- platform-specific impression definition
- observation window
- baseline and target range
- account/channel context
- traffic or distribution source
- secondary quality metric that prevents empty reach

Pair impressions with at least one quality metric such as reading depth, CTR, retention, engagement quality, or conversion.

## Design Questions

- Is there demonstrated audience demand?
- Can the promise be recognized immediately?
- Is the content sufficiently differentiated from competing supply?
- Does the delivered artifact fulfill the entry promise?
- Are there policy, format, or account constraints limiting distribution?

## Evaluation

Before publication, score only proxy conditions. After publication, ingest actual platform analytics and separate topic, packaging, account, timing, and content effects where evidence permits.
