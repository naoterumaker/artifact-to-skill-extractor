# YouTube Platform Adapter

Use this adapter with the video modality and an explicit objective. A "good video" is not a sufficient target.

## Intake

Collect when available:

- channel theme and current audience
- subscriber and returning-viewer context
- typical impressions, CTR, retention, and watch time
- major traffic sources
- successful and failed topics
- competing channels and adjacent videos
- production capacity and publishing constraints

## Impressions Research Axes

Research current evidence for:

- audience demand and channel fit
- topic recognizability and novelty
- search, browse, and suggested-video contexts
- title and thumbnail promise
- title/thumbnail complement rather than duplication
- promise delivery in the opening
- initial response and satisfaction signals

Do not promise impressions. Design and evaluate the conditions that make distribution more plausible, then update from actual analytics.

## Evaluation Split

Pre-publication:

- topic/audience fit
- packaging clarity and differentiation
- title-thumbnail-content promise alignment
- opening delivery and retention risks

Post-publication:

- impressions by source
- CTR by context when available
- early and overall retention
- watch time and satisfaction proxies
- returning viewers and downstream action

Platform mechanics change. Follow `references/research/freshness-policy.md` before encoding current recommendations.
